# THE PROBLEM SECTION - Detailed Build Spec

**For:** Cold Lava Sales Deck  
**Position:** Section 2 (after Hero, before Company)  
**Purpose:** Establish the tension that drives decision-making  
**Tone:** Empathetic but direct. We understand because we've been there.

---

## üéØ THE CORE TENSION

Business owners are stuck between **two opposing fears**:

**Fear 1:** "AI is scary. What if it breaks things? What if I don't understand it? What if I make the wrong choice?"

**Fear 2:** "My competitors are using AI right now and eating my lunch. What if I get left behind?"

**The Result:** Paralysis. They do nothing. And that's the worst option.

**Our Role:** Break the paralysis by being the bridge. Real business people who understand AI, not tech nerds who've never run a business.

## üé® VISUAL DESIGN SPEC

**Color Palette for This Section:**
- **Pain/Fear:** Dark red/orange gradients (vs. usual cyan)
- **Competitor advantage:** Green checkmarks with glow
- **Your current state:** Red X's with subtle pulse
- **The bridge/solution:** Gold accents (Cold Lava color)
- **Background:** Darker than other sections (more dramatic)

**Typography:**
- **Headings:** Bold, direct (not flowery)
- **Body text:** Short sentences. Punchy.
- **Key phrases:** Isolated for impact (use white space)

**Animation Style:**
- Slower, more deliberate than other sections
- Let pain points "land" (2-3s pause between)
- Use subtle "weight" (shake, pulse) to emphasize discomfort
- Transition to gold/hope at the end

**Corner Decorations:**
- Pain/problem cards: Red corners
- Solution/bridge cards: Gold corners
- Maintain Cold Lava grid pattern throughout

## üí¨ PRESENTER NOTES (Sales Rep Training)

### **Delivery Tips**

**Pace:**
- Slow down for this section
- Let the fear resonate
- Don't rush to the solution

**Tone:**
- Empathetic, not preachy
- "We've been there" not "You're doing it wrong"
- Confident but not arrogant

**Watch for:**
- **Nodding heads** - they're feeling the pain, you're connecting
- **Leaning forward** - they want the solution, time to move on
- **Defensive posture** - you've hit a nerve, acknowledge it gently

### **Discovery Questions to Ask**

**After Slide 1 (The Trap):**
"Which side feels more familiar right now - the fear of moving too fast, or falling behind?"

**After Slide 3 (What Real Business Looks Like):**
"Which of these scenarios hits closest to home for you?"

**After Slide 5 (Unfair Advantage):**
"Do you know who your competitors are using for this? What are you hearing?"

### **Objection Handling**

**If they say: "We're not ready for AI yet"**
‚Üí "That's exactly why we're talking. Because when you ARE ready, you want to do it right. Not rush and waste money."

**If they say: "AI is too expensive"**
‚Üí "What's expensive is missing leads. Or spending 15 hours a week on admin. AI pays for itself in weeks, not years."

**If they say: "We tried AI and it didn't work"**
‚Üí "I hear that a lot. What did you try? [LISTEN] That's the problem - off-the-shelf AI doesn't fit real businesses. That's why we build custom."
